Abyssal

Chapter 1 - The Reign of Earl Damien I

January 1, 867 - Dunasead, Desmond

The next thing he knew, Damien was in the year 867 in the southwest of what, in his time, was Ireland. But he didn’t know what the political situation was in 867; he hadn’t paid much attention in history class at school. He looked at the nearby castle, wishing that it would be his. The castle drawbridge lowered, and a beautiful young woman walked across.

“I have been waiting for you, my Earl. My name is Aliisza. Come inside and I’ll explain things.” Damien followed Aliisza into the castle. She told him that his coming was foretold by the Devil and that she was to help him. And with that, Damien del Diablo’s quest for evil began…

Damien’s first task was to banish the Catholic landholders from his realm.

He gave the bishopric to the one person that shared his goals – Aliisza – and kept the city for himself. He also named Aliisza his spymaster; she was the only council member that was serving willingly. The others were Catholics forced to serve under threat of death.

Damien sent his chancellor to fabricate a claim on the neighboring county of Ormond. According to the chancellor, he could attack immediately if he wanted, but then he would have the entire island attacking him. This was not what Damien wanted.

Damien had the rest of his council focus on the interior of his domain: his marshal trained his troops, his steward collected taxes, and Aliisza tried to find any plots against her liege.

January 3, 868

One of Damien’s subjects told him that he would be a better spymaster than Aliisza.

Damien needed a court chaplain, and he thought Aliisza would be perfect for that job. So, knowing that it would make Aliisza upset, he granted the man’s request. However, when Damien asked Aliisza to remain on the council as court chaplain, she refused! He gave her the title of High Almoner, hoping that she would change her mind. But she didn’t.

July 12, 868

This was a bad day for the forces of evil. Their leader, Damien del Diablo, lost one of the Seven Deadly Sins.

August 3, 868

Upset with his chancellor’s lack of progress in claiming Ormond for the Abyssal forces, Damien decided to invade his other neighbor to the north, the county of Thomond – and anyone who tried to stop him would receive their eternal reward in the fires of Hell.

Damien immediately “borrowed” 300 gold from Jewish moneylenders, and then banished them for failing to show proper respect to the Devil.

Sure enough, when the Abyssal troops moved into Thomond, the neighboring counties raised their own levies. Damien used the gold he had “borrowed” to hire mercenaries that either did not know or did not care that they were doing the Devil’s bidding, and sent them to the front lines.

While Damien was sieging Thomond, the Catholic forces had decided to cut the head off the Abyssal snake by sieging Damien’s own castle! Damien couldn’t abandon the siege of Thomond, and right before the castle fell, Aliisza had a son, who she named after her liege.

Luckily, Aliisza and her newborn son were able to escape the fallen castle, and Abyssal forces quickly moved to retake it. The Abyssal and Catholic armies clashed outside the walls of Damien’s castle, and as the Abyssal forces celebrated their victory, Damien got news from Ormond. His chancellor finally had that claim he was supposed to get two years earlier! Damien made a mental note to punish the chancellor for his tardiness.

With the victory in Thomond, will Damien be able to keep taking land?
