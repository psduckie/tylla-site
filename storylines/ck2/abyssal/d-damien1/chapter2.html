Abyssal

Chapter 2 - The Reign of King Damien I

November 9, 869

With Thomond safely in Abyssal hands, Damien decided that he should no longer be referred to as “Earl.” Instead, he should be known as “King!”

The newly-crowned King granted Thomond’s bishopric to one of his loyal followers, and made that man his Court Chaplain, a position that had until that time been vacant.

Damien immediately set his new Chaplain to the task of converting his lands to the Abyssal ways, and set his own sights on the neighboring provinces. He would stop at nothing to claim them as his own, and his royal decree that the Abyssal Church would provide him with as many forces as it could showed that!

As part of his “stop-at-nothing” strategy, Damien hired mercenaries that he could not afford for an attack on Ormond.

Needless to say, the attack failed when Damien ran out of gold and the mercenaries left. Worse, while Damien was on the battlefield, two disturbing things happened. First, he received a foreboding message…

And second, he took an arrow not in the knee, but just below. According to some of the more seasoned combatants, if the arrow had hit just a bit higher, Damien would have never been able to fight again. As it was, it would leave a scar, but it would heal.

(Chronicler’s note: I couldn’t resist the opportunity for a Skyrim joke, even though Skyrim didn’t exist in 2006!)

Surprisingly, Ormond accepted a white peace, even though they had clearly won the war. But Damien swore that he would one day rule that land.

December 29, 870

The Spymaster that had been appointed back in 868 had since been fired for poor performance, and Aliisza had been given the job back. Once again, he claimed to Damien that he would make a better Spymaster than Aliisza. This time, Damien told him that he was still fired, and that if he asked again, he would get a free trip to the dungeons. As the man left, Damien wondered if he had done the right thing in giving him a second chance. Aliisza told Damien that if the man had indeed learned his lesson, then the answer was yes.

May 5, 871

Two people got married in the Catholic tradition. This was highly illegal in the Abyssal realm. To make matters worse, one of them was Damien’s Chancellor! Damien ordered both newlyweds captured and imprisoned, but using the Chancellor’s knowledge of Damien’s own escape routes, they escaped from the Abyssal realm.

June 9, 871

Aliisza was once again pregnant. Damien took the news the same as he had taken the news of her last pregnancy: with sheer enjoyment.

July 3, 871

After the marriage debacle, the Chancellor’s position had been empty for almost two months. Damien finally announced the replacement Chancellor: out of all of the poorly-qualified candidates, Damien picked the least poorly-qualified.

(Chronicler’s note: Yes, I think a 10 is poorly qualified. His skill raised to 11 after I picked him but before I took the picture.)

December 7, 871

Damien had almost forgotten about the message he had received during the disastrous war for Ormond. Almost, but not quite. So he was not surprised when Lorcán, the man that he had been warned about, declared war on him.

The question was, who would win? The evil forces of the Abyssal, or the righteous Lorcán’s Host?

It wasn’t even a question. The war was over within a month. Lorcán’s 1,800-man-strong force completely overpowered the Abyssal forces, which were still weakened from Ormond. Lorcán took Thomond for his own, but left Damien his stronghold of Desmond.

Shortly after the loss of Thomond, Aliisza gave birth to a baby daughter, whom she named after herself.

Will Damien recover from these losses and triumph over his newest enemy?
